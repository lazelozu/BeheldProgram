Prologue: The Passing of the Rite

The Passing of The Rite collectively refers to two stories, The Rite and The Passing. In The Rite, a girl and her cousin flee from their village and learn to live with nature and with each other. The Passing explains the significance of The Rite’s myth, through conflicting accounts. Both stories reveal the early history of the world of Beheld.

Beheld

Beheld unveils the story of two sisters, Cecilia and Camilla, as they navigate the structure and secrecy of an all-women’s religious society, The Order of The Masterhood. Drawn to the neighboring town and its inhabitants, Camilla decides to pursue her own adventure, to unintended consequences. The rift between the sisters results in a shocking journey for Cecilia, as she deals with the effects of Camilla’s actions. Family, love, honor, and the meaning of healing lie at the core of this starkly vivid one-act play.

World
What is The Order of The Masterhood?

The Order of The Masterhood collectively refers to the social classes within the all-women’s religious society, The Masterhood and The Order. The Masterhood—comprised of the Sole Mothers, the Mistresses, and the Sisters—is an advanced, literate, and independent society in which women and girls follow and commit strict rites and responsibilities, known as talents, to support themselves and their trade with outsiders. The Masterhood is led by three Sole Mothers, who recruit new Sisters and servants, oversee rites and events, manage trade, and educate the Mistresses and Sisters. The nine Mistresses support The Masterhood by arranging rites, facilitating trade, creating more advanced treatments/herbal medicine, and sometimes teaching their Sisters. The thirty Sisters aid these efforts through creating treatments and assisting with agriculture. The Order, composed of numerous servants, serve and sustain the operations and members of The Masterhood.
